Solution 3

Abstract:

The suggested energy harvester is built on a backpack, and it integrates seamlessly with the backpack's bulk to produce an oscillating system, allowing the human body trunk's kinetic motion to be captured and used to power a generator. As a backpack usually has two leg straps to increase the output efficiency, the energy harvester contains two sets of harvesting mechanisms, which are symmetrically constructed, as shown in figure 1.

Mechanism:

A pulling wire with a hanger, as well as a roller, help compensate for each harvesting mechanism: a micro-generator, a gear train, and a wheel with an integrated spiral spring. The hanger is linked to one of the backpack's straps, and it drives the pulling wire and turns the roller wheel when the backpack oscillates during human walking.
Because human motion has a low frequency (approximately 1 Hz), the angular speed of the roller wheel is much lower than the speed desired for the generator to work efficiently; thus, a gear train is used to accelerate the rotation of the roller wheel and then drive the generator to produce electricity.
The spiral spring, coaxial with the roller wheel, acts as a restoring force for the pulling wire and is the energy harvester's final main component of the oscillation system. Except for the pulling wire and its hanger, all of the above components are combined in a case, which is attached to the backpack such that the weight of the harvester and the mass stored in the backpack serves as the seismic mass of the oscillation system.
As illustrated in figure 2, the energy harvester case, which contains the majority of the components, is linked to the backpack container. The hangers are connected to the backpack's straps, allowing relative movement between the backpack container's straps. This relative movement drives the generators.
Because the two generators' electrical outputs are irregular, a full-wave bridge rectification circuit is used to correct the power and generate a direct current, which is then temporarily stored in a battery. The collected electrical power from the battery may be utilized to charge a variety of portable electronic devices through a charging circuit and USB power connectors. Portable electronic gadgets may be powered indefinitely with this energy harvester instead of carrying a bulky battery with a large capacity.
